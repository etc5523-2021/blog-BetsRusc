Kazakhstan: Summers are for infection

Kazakhstan, like the rest of the world, has been a country deeply effected by the COVID-19 pandemic. Their infection rates tell a story of 2020 devastation, followed by a 2021 catastrophe.

Aside from the huge surge of cases in 2021 overall, it is worth noting that the deadlier months tend to be in the Summer - with the most number of cases cropping up in July and August 2021, and with many deaths in both August 2020 and August 2021.

This can be shown by playing around with the arrows at the top of the New_Cases and New_Deaths columns in the first table.

Vaccinations

In combating the virus, Kazakhstan started their vaccination rollout this year in April.

Vaccination rates have brought them from 7% vaccinated to 34% vaccinated in 5 months, as seen by the final column in the table below.
